warm Pyrrhic victory. It comes from King Pyrrhus of Epirus, a Greek king who fought the Romans around 280 BC. He won the battles - technically. But he lost so many of his own men in the process that after one particularly brutal "victory" he reportedly said: "If we are victorious in one more battle with the Romans, we shall be utterly ruined." A pyrrhic victory is when you win, but the cost of winning is so high that it's basically indistinguishable from losing.
